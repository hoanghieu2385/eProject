RAINWATER HARVESTING IN RESIDENTIAL AREA

What Are The Uses Of Collected Water?


You can essentially use rainwater anywhere you use tap water. The idea of using drinking water to flush our toilets and water our lawns is wasteful and irresponsible, especially in light of population growth and water shortages across the country. Rainwater collection is a technique to green your home and to lessen your environmental footprint. There are basically three areas where rainwater can be used: Irrigation use Indoor, non-potable use Whole house, potable use Here are some ideas for specific uses of rainwater: Hand water your lawn and garden Connect rainwater collection system to irrigation/sprinkler system Wash your vehicles Wash your pets Refill your fountains and fish ponds Refill your swimming pool Replace the use of tap water with rainwater to wash your driveways and sidewalks (if you don’t use a broom) Use it for all indoor non-potable fixtures (toilets and clothes washer) Use it for all potable needs when properly filtered and disinfected Use it for industrial processes instead of municipally treated water

Residential rainwater harvesting systems

1. Rainwater Filtration Systems

Rainwater filters are crucial components in commercial rainwater harvesting systems, designed to remove debris, sediments, and contaminants from collected rainwater. High-quality filters ensure the harvested water meets quality standards for various applications.

2. Storage Tanks with UV Treatment:

Commercial rainwater harvesting systems often incorporate storage tanks equipped with ultraviolet (UV) treatment technology. UV treatment helps eliminate harmful microorganisms and ensures the stored rainwater remains safe for use in irrigation, plumbing, or other purposes.

3. Smart Rainwater Harvesting Controllers:

Smart controllers are an innovative addition to commercial rainwater harvesting systems. These devices use advanced sensors and automation to optimize the harvesting process. They can adjust collection rates based on weather forecasts, ensuring maximum efficiency and water conservation.
