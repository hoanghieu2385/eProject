RAINWATER HARVESTING IN RESIDENTIAL AREA

What Are The Uses Of Collected Rainwater?

Rainwater collection is a sustainable practice that helps conserve drinking water. It can be used for irrigation, non-potable indoor activities, and even as potable water with proper filtration. This eco-friendly approach reduces water wastage and environmental impact. Specific uses include watering gardens, connecting to irrigation systems, washing vehicles and pets, filling fountains and pools, and replacing tap water for various purposes, contributing to a greener home and a smaller ecological footprint.

RESIDENTIAL RAINWATER HARVESTING SYSTEMS

1. Rain Barrels

Description: Rain barrels are containers designed to collect and store rainwater from rooftops. They often come equipped with a filter system to remove debris and contaminants.

Benefits: Easy to install, cost-effective, and environmentally friendly. They help conserve water and reduce the demand on municipal water supplies.

2. First Flush Diverters

Description: First flush diverters are devices that divert the initial flow of rainwater away from the collection system. This helps to discard the first flush of water which may contain pollutants and contaminants.

Benefits: Improves the quality of collected rainwater by preventing the accumulation of pollutants from the roof surface.

3. Water Pumps

Description: Water pumps are essential for distributing harvested rainwater for various uses, such as watering the garden or flushing toilets. They come in various types, including submersible and surface pumps.

Benefits: Ensures efficient use of harvested rainwater by providing a reliable means of water distribution.
